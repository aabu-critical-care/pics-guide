What is PICS?

[span_0](start_span)

Definition: PICS refers to new or worsening impairments in physical, cognitive, or mental health status arising after critical illness and persisting beyond acute care hospitalization[span_0](end_span).

[span_1](start_span)

Impact: Approximately 50â€“70% of ICU survivors experience at least one PICS impairment[span_1](end_span).

Physical Cognitive Psychological

Physical Impairments

[span_2](start_span)

Primarily related to ICU-acquired weakness and muscle wasting[span_2](end_span).

  • Generalized weakness & fatigue.
    • [span_3](start_span)
  • Difficulty with activities of daily living (ADLs)[span_3](end_span).
  • Reduced exercise tolerance.

Cognitive Impairments

[span_4](start_span)

Associated with hypoxia, delirium, and metabolic disturbances[span_4](end_span). Symptoms include:

  • Memory deficits.
    • [span_5](start_span)
  • Reduced attention span[span_5](end_span).
  • Slower mental processing speed.

Psychological Impairments

[span_6](start_span)

Arise from the traumatic nature of critical illness[span_6](end_span). Common issues:

    [span_7](start_span)
  • Anxiety & Depression[span_7](end_span).
  • PTSD (Nightmares, Flashbacks).
[span_8](start_span)

Note: This can also affect family members (PICS-Family)[span_8](end_span).

Management & Nursing Role

[span_9](start_span)

Management requires a multidisciplinary approach including early mobilization and patient education[span_9](end_span).
